### Out at Sea
I spent much of the second half of 2015 on an oil rig in the southern North Sea. There was no land in sight. 

It was a strange time for me. I was preparing to leave the UK and move to Australia for an uncertain future. On the rig we didn't have (usable) internet. We had a weak satellite connection used by our workstations and split between 200 people. We also had the occasional rogue Facetime user who would attempt to call home and take out the entire network.

As a result, I would spend a fair bit of my non-work time out on deck just staring into the distance dramatically. I grew up with the sea visible from my bedroom window, so staring at the sea is at the very least a habit, if not something core to who I am.

Out at Sea is a dumb web experiment but also possibly a way for me to step back into the specific in-between moment I found myself in 11 years ago. It is a quiet digital space meant for pausing and letting your mind drift.

Most of this code is out-of-the-box [three.js](https://threejs.org). I am thankful for the countless thousands of person-hours which have gone into building common-good open source software like this. I am also continually impressed by what is possible and achievable in modern browsers. 

#### Interaction
* Look around: Click and drag (or swipe) left and right to look around the full 360-degree horizon. 
* Change the time of day: Drag up and down to move the sun.

#### Built with:
* Three.js (WebGL): Handles the 3D space, camera math and animations.
* Water and Sky Shaders: Built-in extensions for Three.js.
* Web Audio API: Background wind sound is made from random noise with digital filters.
